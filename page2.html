CAMP WAINUIOMATA

203 Coast Road, Wainuiomata, Wellington 5373

Camp Wainui is a fantastic camping site known for its stunning scenery and exciting range of outdoor activities they provide. Surrounded by beautiful terrain, where students will learn how to pitch their tents, dive into new challenges and spend time with their friends. Over the course of two days, you'll have the chance to a variety of activities. This included raft building, tramping and team challenges that will test your creativity and cooperation. There’s something for everyone, and an opportunity for students to make beautiful memories. Camp Wainuiomata isn’t just a place to visit, it’s a place to explore, bond, and have lots of fun!

DAY 1

Students will congregate with their form class. They will need to have his day pack/ backpack with lunch, water bottle, sun hat, a warm jumper/thermal and rain jacket. In addition he will have one overnight bag, with the gear listed below, and a tent if you are providing one. The day pack must be separate from the overnight bag. Please wear appropriate sports clothing with lace up sports shoes with good grip. No denim please. Bring your own medication if required.


Once the roll has been taken we will catch a private bus to Wainui.


Students will be taken straight to camp Wainui where he will first construct a tent with his friends and put his overnight bag inside. We have a large camping area at the back of the camp as well as the use of the hall and kitchen and toilets in the huts.


There are a few showers available but we would strongly discourage the use of the showers where possible as it takes a while for 260 people to shower and we are only away for one night.

DAY 2

We will wake nice and early and have a breakfast of cereals, toast and spreads.


Tents will be packed down and overnight bags packed. These will be left in a pile ready for the buses to pick them up in the afternoon.


Students will take their day pack with their second lunch and a water bottle, rain jacket and warm top/hat and board the bus to go 7kms down the road to the Catchpool carpark in the Remutaka Valley Park. From here he will walk the Orongorongo track in and out of the valley (5.2km each way). Please note, there is a track detour right at the end of the track due to a potential slip - we will be taking the detour that DOC has provided. On the way Students will learn some map reading skills and about local flora and fauna.


Once in the valley, students will have lunch and also:
* Learn how to build their own hobo stoves for cooking in the bush.
* If the river is at its usual height of up to calf depth and not fast flowing, we will learn about river crossings. If there has been more rainfall than usual and the river is deeper or more fast flowing, this will be cancelled.

Then we will head back along the track to the car park where a private bus will pick us up and transport us back to school for around 3.30pm.

! IMPORTANT !

Cell phone coverage at camp is very limited. In an emergency, if you can't reach your child on their mobile, please contact the school during hours or Camp Wainui after hours at (04) 564 5305. Charging points are scarce, so we recommend students bring a power bank or keep phones on aeroplane mode to save battery. Devices should not be used during camp, but phones can be turned on upon return to school if needed for pickup arrangements.

Caregivers agreed in the permission slip to discuss camp rules with their child, including no alcohol, vaping, smoking, or drugs, and to follow all rules. Violating these rules will result in a call for pickup and possible school consequences. After lights out, students must remain in their tents
