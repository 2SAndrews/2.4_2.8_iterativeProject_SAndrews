THE CHANCE TO GIVE BACK OUR COMMUNITY

AT TAWA COLLEGE

Community service is a meaningful part of our EOTC week, offering us a valuable opportunity to give back to the community that has supported us in so many ways. It’s our chance to show appreciation , whether it’s through helping local organisations, cleaning up shared spaces, or lending a hand to those in need. By stepping up and contributing our time and effort, we not only strengthen our ties with the community but also grow as individuals, learning the importance of our school values.

YOUR HELP BRINGS JOY TO OTHERS

KEY INFORMATION

Students will complete one day of community service assisting local residents and businesses. This will usually occur within walking distance of Tawa College for all Year 10 students. This day has the key focus of citizenship and has been a great success in previous years.


Students will spend a day completing community service in a small group with friends in the Tawa basin, supervised by a staff member. This is a highly valuable experience where students have the chance to interact with the elderly helping them with gardening, painting etc or assisting local food banks, kindergartens, hospice shops and charities for the day. Students will usually walk to their allocated job with a staff member, however a very small number of jobs will be on the fringes of the Tawa Basin and therefore they may be driven by a staff member in their groups.
